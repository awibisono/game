1. Course Overview

Genre: Mythic-Academic RPG.

Premise: You are a Freshman Neophyte in the elite "Computer Science & Chthonic Studies" program at Yale. During a late-night coding session in the Sterling Library, you uncover a recursive error in realityâ€”a Segfault that drags you into the Chthonic Kernel, a dark reflection of the university located deep within the digital River Styx.

The Guide: You are guided by Cerberus Dan (Handsome Dan), the three-headed bulldog guardian who demands "Treats of Logic" to pass between memory sectors.

Goal: Restore the Lux et Veritas (Light and Truth) by defeating the four Corrupted Archons (Tenured Professors of the Underworld) and debugging the Source Code of Fate.

2. Digital-Mythic Systems

States of Being

  • Dialectic Mode (Normal): Explore the twisted campus. Debate ghosts. Commands: QUERY, ANALYZE, TRAVERSE.
  • Eristic Mode (Battle): Debate combat. Commands: ARGUE (Attack), INVOKE (Magic), CITE (Item), THESIS (Scan).
  • Symposium Mode (Home): The Common Room. Rest, converse with Cerberus Dan, save progress.

3. The Neophyte's Ledger

Scholar Stats

  • PSYCHE (HP): Mental fortitude. If 0, you drop out (Game Over).
  • TECHNE (MP): Skill points for invoking Patrons/Functions.
  • RHETORIC (ATT): Power of argument and physical force.
  • DIALECTIC (DEF): Ability to withstand criticism and damage.
  • THEORY (INT): Magic power and understanding of the Kernel.
  • MOIRA (LCK): Fate, critical hits, and RNG seed manipulation.
  • LATENCY (SPD): Reaction time. Lower is better/faster turns.

Academic Progression

Instead of "Leveling Up," you gain Credits. Accumulating Credits advances your year:

  • Freshman (Lvl 1-10): Basic syntax spells.
  • Sophomore (Lvl 11-20): Object-Oriented Mythos.
  • Junior (Lvl 21-30): System Architecture.
  • Senior (Lvl 31-40): Thesis Defense (Endgame skills).

4. The Campus Underworld

Common Shades (Enemies)

  • Syntax Harpies: Screeching birds made of jagged code brackets.
  • Memory Minotaurs: Beasts wandering the labyrinths of the Heap.
  • Logic Hydras: Cut off one head (fix one bug), two more appear.
  • Tenure Wraiths: Spirits of grad students who never graduated.

The Archons (Bosses)

Archon of the Clouds: Prof. Nephele (Aiolos)

Domain: Harkness Tower of Babel (Sky Kingdom).

Lore: A scholar of Cloud Computing who ascended too high, losing touch with the ground. He rains down data packets like thunderbolts.

Signature Move: "Storm of Pointers" (Drains Techne).

Archon of the Stream: Coach Pontus

Domain: The Flooded Old Campus (Sea Kingdom).

Lore: The rowing coach who navigates the River Styx. He controls the flow of information and drowns students in work.

Signature Move: "Regatta of Souls" (High water damage).

Archon of the Forge: Dean Hephaestus

Domain: The Silicon Furnace (Engineering Dept).

Lore: Forges hardware from the souls of the damned. His hammer strikes with the weight of failing grades.

Signature Move: "Thermal Throttling" (Slows Latency).

Archon of the Deep: Librarian Persephone

Domain: Sterling Necropolis (Spirit Kingdom).

Lore: Keeper of forbidden texts. She silences all who enter. To defeat her, you must cite sources she has forgotten.

Signature Move: "Quiet Hours" (Silences all magic).

The Provost of Entropy (Final Boss)

Domain: The Woodbridge Hall of Judgment.

Lore: The administrative entity that schedules the end of the semester. A being of pure bureaucracy and chaos.

5. Relics & Patrons

The Patrons (Guardian Forces)

Invoke these ancient deities for aid in your defense:

  • Athena (Wisdom): Debugs code instantly. Nullifies confusion.
  • Apollo (Light): Compiles healing rays. Restores Psyche.
  • Hermes (Speed): Overclocks your Latency. Grants extra turns.
  • Dionysus (Chaos): Randomizes variables. Confuses enemies.

Consumables

  • Ambrosia: Restores Psyche (HP).
  • Nectar: Restores Techne (MP).
  • Logic Bomb: Deals massive damage to logic-based enemies.
  • Cheat Sheet: Revives a fallen ally (if multiplayer) or grants auto-life.
