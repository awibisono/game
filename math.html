The Mathematics of the Eigen-Sea

The movement of every Fungible (agent) in this simulation is governed by the McKean-Vlasov Stochastic Differential Equation (SDE). This equation models the behavior of particles interacting in a mean-field limit.

$$ dX_t = \underbrace{-\nabla V(X_t) dt}_{\text{Confinement}} - \underbrace{\frac{1}{N} \sum_{j=1}^N \nabla W(X_t - X_j) dt}_{\text{Interaction}} + \underbrace{\sqrt{2\sigma} dB_t}_{\text{Exploration}} $$

1. The Confinement Potential \( V(x) \)

This term forces agents towards specific regions (food, objectives).

  • Quadratic (Gravity): \( V(x) = \frac{1}{2}k|x|^2 \). Forces convergence to the origin.
  • Rastrigin (Chaos): \( V(x) = 10d + \sum (x_i^2 - 10\cos(2\pi x_i)) \). Creates many local traps.

2. The Interaction Kernel \( W(x-y) \)

This defines the "Society" of the agents.

  • Coulomb (Repulsion): \( W(r) = -k/|r| \). Agents spread out to minimize energy (Crystal formation).
  • Newtonian (Attraction): \( W(r) = k|r| \). Agents clump together (Collapse).
  • Morse (Swarm): \( W(r) = C_a e^{-r/L_a} - C_r e^{-r/L_r} \). Short-range repulsion, long-range attraction. Creates biological swarming patterns.

3. Special Cases

Case A: \( W = 0 \) (The Ideal Gas)

If interaction is zero, the agents become independent Ornstein-Uhlenbeck processes. They explore the landscape \( V \) without seeing each other. The stationary distribution is the Boltzmann distribution: \( \rho(x) \propto e^{-V(x)/\sigma} \).

Case B: \( \sigma = 0 \) (Gradient Descent)

If noise is zero, the system becomes a deterministic gradient flow. It will freeze at a local minimum of the total energy functional \( \mathcal{E}(\mu) \).
