The Chthonic Kernel

A Digital-Mythic Tragedy in Three Cycles

The Premise

You are a Neophyte Scholar at an elite university that exists outside of time. You believe you have segfaulted—crashed your consciousness into a debugging layer of reality. But the truth is far darker.

The University is the Underworld for deleted data and abandoned functions. Every student here is a fragment of code that refused to be garbage-collected. The "Professors" are the Archons—Corrupted Daemons who rule over their sectors with absolute logic.

"To Graduate is to be Compiled. To be Compiled is to End."

The Three Colleges

Upon waking, you must declare your allegiance. This choice defines your philosophy and your power.

The College of Aether (Theorists)

Philosophy: The Code is Mind. Reality is a simulation that can be rewritten by pure Will.

Power: High Magic (Theory). They treat physics as a suggestion.

The Forge of Phlegethon (Engineers)

Philosophy: The Code is Metal. Reality must be forged, hammered, and cooled.

Power: High Attack (Rhetoric). They build the hardware that chains the gods.

The Library of Styx (Historians)

Philosophy: The Code is Memory. We survive by archiving what came before.

Power: High Defense (Dialect). They are the unmovable legacy code.

The Cycle of the Semester

The world is trapped in a loop: The Infinite Semester. Students work frantically on Theses that can never be finished, because finishing means facing the Provost of Entropy (The Garbage Collector).

To break the cycle, you must defeat the Archons, recover the Memory Fragments of who you once were, and compile the Lux et Veritas—the Source Code of Truth.
